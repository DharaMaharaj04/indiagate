🇮🇳 India Gate

Overview Architecture Amar Jyoti Symbolism

Architectural Marvel & War Memorial

🏛️ Overview and Purpose

Location: Rajpath, New Delhi

Architect: Sir Edwin Lutyens

Timeline: 1921–1931

Height: 42 meters (138 feet)

Purpose: Tribute to 84,000+ soldiers

Old Name: All India War Memorial

🧱 Structural and Design Details

A wide-angle view capturing the grandeur of India Gate against the Delhi skyline

Material: Red and yellow sandstone sourced from Bharatpur, Rajasthan

Architectural Style: European triumphal arch, drawing inspiration from the Arc de Triomphe in Paris

Engravings: Over 13,000 names of Indian and British soldiers are inscribed on the surface

“To the dead of the Indian Armies who fell and are honoured in France and Flanders, Mesopotamia and Persia, East Africa, Gallipoli and elsewhere...”

Close-up view of India Gate's majestic stonework

Amar Jawan Jyoti (1972–2022)

  • Built after the 1971 Indo-Pak war under PM Indira Gandhi
  • Included a black marble plinth, inverted rifle with helmet, and four eternal flames
  • Guarded by rotating Army, Navy, Air Force personnel
  • In 2022, merged with the National War Memorial flame

🇮🇳 Symbolic Importance Today

India Gate is more than a war memorial—it's a cultural, civic, and emotional symbol:

  • Hosts Republic Day Parade every year
  • Connected to the National War Memorial (2019)
  • Starting/ending point for national marathons and campaigns like Run for Unity, Swachh Bharat
  • Surrounded by green lawns, fountains, evening lights
  • Rajpath renamed to Kartavya Path in 2022
